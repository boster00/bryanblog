What is this blog?

This is a free, independent retirement-planning resource. It covers the questions most people start asking somewhere between age 55 and 75: When should I claim Social Security? How do I make sure my money doesn’t run out before I do? What’s the smartest way to draw down savings?

The goal is simple: give you clear, balanced information so you can make better decisions—on your own or with a financial professional. Nothing is sold here. No product is pushed.

Think of it as the friend who happened to spend years reading everything written about retirement income and is now willing to sit down and walk you through it, without the sales pitch.

Who is this blog for?

This blog is written with one reader in mind: someone in their late 50s, 60s, or early 70s who is getting serious about retirement and wants to understand their options before committing to anything.

You will likely get the most from this site if you:

  • Are within 10 years of retirement, already retired, or helping a spouse plan
  • Feel like financial content is either too basic or too full of jargon
  • Want to understand why a strategy works, not just what to do
  • Are skeptical of advice that always seems to end in a product recommendation
  • Prefer reading at your own pace over attending webinars or sales seminars

You do not need to be a numbers person. The writing here assumes no financial background—only that you care about getting this right.

What makes it different from other retirement sites?

Most retirement content you find online falls into one of two camps: it’s either so general it tells you nothing useful, or it’s secretly a lead-generation page for an annuity, a brokerage, or an insurance company.

This blog tries to be neither of those things. A few things set it apart:

  • No ads, no affiliate links, no sponsored content. The writing is not shaped by who is paying for a banner or a referral fee.
  • Tradeoffs are shown honestly. Every strategy has downsides. This blog names them plainly instead of burying them in fine print.
  • Plain language throughout. Financial terms are explained the first time they appear. The goal is comprehension, not credibility through complexity.
  • No specific products are recommended. You will learn how a category of product works and what questions to ask—not which brand to buy.
  • Written for your stage of life. The examples, the concerns, and the tone all reflect someone who is closer to the finish line than the starting block.

How is the content organized?

The site is built around two layers: pillar guides and deep-dive articles.

The foundation

Each pillar guide covers a major topic end-to-end—retirement income, Social Security timing, healthcare costs, and more. They are designed to be read once, top to bottom, to give you a solid footing.

The detail

Deep-dive articles branch off each pillar guide to explore a narrower question in full. They are linked in context so you can follow whatever thread matters most to your situation.

This structure means you can start broad and go as deep as you need, without wading through content that does not apply to you.

Example: The retirement income pillar guide walks through the key income sources in retirement and how they fit together. From there, linked articles cover each source in more depth—sequence-of-returns risk, safe withdrawal strategies, income flooring, and so on.

What is this blog NOT?

It is worth being clear about what this site does not do, so there are no surprises.

  • Not a financial advisor. Nothing here is personalized financial advice. It is general education. Always work with a licensed professional before making major decisions.
  • Not a product comparison site. You will not find star ratings for insurance companies or brokerage accounts. That is not the purpose.
  • Not a news site. The focus is on concepts and strategies that remain useful over years, not on market moves or weekly headlines.
  • Not a community or forum. There is no comment section or membership. It is a library, not a social platform.
  • Not a source of return projections. Any numbers used in examples are for illustration only. Real results vary—sometimes significantly.

If you are looking for a place to get personalized advice, this is not it. If you are looking for a place to understand your options before you seek that advice, you are in the right spot.

How do I get started?

The best place to begin is with the pillar guide on retirement income. It lays out the core questions—what income sources you will have, how to sequence them, and how to think about making your money last. From there, detailed deep-dive articles branch out to cover each piece in more depth.

You do not need to read everything. Find the section that matches where you are right now, and start there. Most articles take 10 to 15 minutes to read. There is no right order beyond the one that fits your current questions.
